8 sample questions with worked answers

We wrote these to match the real mix: lookups, calculations, and theory. Every calculation below was checked by hand; we reworked the box fill twice before it reconciled.

Question 1: Ohm's law

A 1,200 W heater runs on a 120 V circuit. Find the current and the resistance.

Worked answer: I = P / E = 1,200 / 120 = 10 A. R = E / I = 120 / 10 = 12 ohms. Cross-check with R = E squared / P = 14,400 / 1,200 = 12 ohms. Same answer both ways, so it reconciles.

Question 2: series circuit

Three resistors of 10, 20, and 30 ohms are in series across 120 V. What is the circuit current?

Worked answer: Series resistance adds: 10 + 20 + 30 = 60 ohms. I = 120 / 60 = 2 A. The same 2 A flows through every resistor; the voltage divides (20 V, 40 V, 60 V, which sums back to 120 V as a check).

Question 3: transformer ratio

A transformer steps 480 V down to 120 V. If the primary draws 5 A, what does the secondary deliver?

Worked answer: Turns ratio is 480:120 = 4:1. Current is inverse to voltage, so secondary current = 5 x 4 = 20 A. Power check: 480 x 5 = 2,400 VA in, 120 x 20 = 2,400 VA out. It balances.

Question 4: box fill per NEC 314.16

A box contains six 14 AWG circuit conductors, two equipment grounding conductors, and one switch. What volume is required?

Worked answer: 14 AWG counts at 2.00 cu in per allowance. Conductors: 6 x 2.00 = 12.00. All EGCs together are one allowance: 2.00. The switch yoke counts double: 2 x 2.00 = 4.00. Total = 18.00 cu in. A 4 in square, 1-1/2 in deep box (21.0 cu in) works; a 3 x 2 x 3-1/2 device box (18.0 cu in) is exactly at the limit.

Question 5: conduit fill

How many 12 AWG THHN conductors fit in trade size 3/4 EMT?

Worked answer: 3/4 EMT internal area is 0.533 sq in; at 40% fill that is 0.2132 sq in. THHN 12 AWG is 0.0133 sq in each. 0.2132 / 0.0133 = 16.03, so 16 conductors. Annex C, Table C.1 confirms 16. Never round 16.03 up; fractional conductors round down unless the 0.8 rule for a single-size calculation applies.

Question 6: ampacity and the small-conductor rule

What is the maximum overcurrent protection for 10 AWG copper THWN on a general-purpose branch circuit?

Worked answer: Table 310.16 gives 10 AWG THWN 35 A at 75 deg C, but 240.4(D) caps 10 AWG copper at a 30 A overcurrent device for general applications. Answer: 30 A. The 35 A table value still matters for derating math, which is exactly why exams love this question.

Question 7: ambient temperature correction

A 12 AWG THHN conductor runs through a 100 deg F attic space. What is its corrected ampacity?

Worked answer: 12 AWG THHN is 30 A in the 90 deg C column. The correction factor for 100 deg F ambient on 90 deg C insulation is 0.91. So 30 x 0.91 = 27.3 A. Since 27.3 exceeds the 20 A limit that 240.4(D) puts on 12 AWG anyway, the circuit still lands on a 20 A breaker.

Question 8: standard breaker sizes

A calculated load requires overcurrent protection of at least 42 A. What standard size breaker applies?

Worked answer: 240.6(A) lists standard ratings: ...35, 40, 45, 50... Since 42 A is not standard, and 240.4(B) permits the next size up for loads under 800 A (where the conductor rules allow), the answer is 45 A.

PDF vs live practice: an honest comparison

We sell study material, so read this knowing our bias, but the mechanics are simple. A PDF is frozen the day it is exported. The NEC is not. How to vet any question PDF in five minutes

1.  Find the NEC edition on the first page. No edition named means assume it is stale and move on.

2.  Spot-check one ampacity answer against the current Table 310.16. A single mismatch condemns the whole file.

3.  Look for worked solutions. Answer letters without method teach recognition, not the skill the exam tests.

4.  Count the calculation questions. A set that is 90% definitions will not prepare you for the math block.

5.  Check the GFCI questions. If the location list looks short, the file predates recent cycles.

A two-week plan that works: week one, do 20 questions a day untimed with the code book, reworking every miss by hand the same evening. Week two, switch to timed 25-question blocks and keep a running list of the tables you had to hunt for, then tab those. Candidates who follow this usually see their timed scores rise 10 to 15 points between the first and last block, mostly from faster navigation rather than new knowledge.

FAQ

Is there a free journeyman electrician practice test PDF?

Free PDFs exist, but quality is uneven and many are keyed to old NEC editions. Before using one, check that it names NEC 2026, shows worked solutions rather than bare answer letters, and covers calculations, not just definitions. A maintained online quiz avoids the staleness problem entirely, and ours is free.

How many questions are on the journeyman exam?

Most states run 60 to 100 multiple-choice questions with 3 to 5 hours of open-book time, and a passing score around 70 to 75 percent. Exact counts, fees, and time limits are set by your state board and its test vendor, so pull the candidate bulletin for your state before scheduling.

What calculations should I practice most?

Box fill per 314.16, conduit fill from Chapter 9 Tables 4 and 5, ampacity with correction and adjustment factors from 310.15, simple appliance and motor loads, and Ohm's law variations. These families appear on nearly every exam, and each has a fixed method that gets fast with repetition.

Do the answers change when the NEC updates?

Some do. Each three-year cycle adjusts tables, expands requirements like GFCI coverage, and renumbers sections. A question written against an old edition may now have a different correct answer or a moved code reference. That is the core weakness of static PDFs and the reason we key our quiz to the current edition.
